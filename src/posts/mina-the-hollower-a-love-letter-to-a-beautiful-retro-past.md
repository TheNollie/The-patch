Mina the Hollower is a masterclass in how to execute a retro throwback game. In an era where modern pixel-art titles frequently miss the mark by either clinging too tightly to the past or overcomplicating their mechanics, this game finds the absolute sweet spot. It does not just mimic the classics; it respects them while creating something entirely its own.

The brilliance of the game lies in how beautifully it blends the worlds of Zelda, Castlevania, and modern Souls-likes. Rather than feeling like a disjointed checklist of genres, these influences harmonize perfectly. The overworld layout and clever puzzles feel like a direct, loving nod to A Link to the Past, forcing you to think about your surroundings and explore every nook and cranny.

When it comes to combat and progression, the game leans heavily into its gothic action roots. The sub-weapons and throwables immediately call back to the best of Castlevania, giving you crucial tactical options during tough encounters. This is paired with a tense, Souls-like risk-and-reward system where you collect bones to level up your character. The constant tension of managing your currency adds a fantastic layer of weight to every encounter, making survival feel earned.

If there is any real critique to be made, it is that the game length runs a bit on the shorter side. However, the developers respect your time, and what the game lacks in sheer hours, it more than makes up for in replayability and customization. The game features a massive variety of modifiers that allow you to tailor the gameplay, difficulty, and experience exactly to your personal liking.

Mina the Hollower is an exceptional experience that reminds us why we fell in love with top-down action games in the first place. For its incredible atmosphere, tight mechanics, and brilliant genre fusion, it easily earns a 9 out of 10.
